The roll of the dice!

The D6 is the dice you're most used to if you've played any sort of board game. From Mouse Trap to Risk to Arkham Horror, it is the quintessential die shape. It is also used fairly heavily within D&D, but unless you're a rogue or a spellcaster you probably won't come across it too often.

The D8 is an interesting little number, typically used for one handed weapon damage, but it is also used by monks at higher levels. It's one of the most commonly used die in D&D as many classes prefer one handed weapons, as they give the freedom to use a shield... or to wield a second weapon!

The D10, again it is used mostly for determining the damage dealt by weapons and spells. But it has another use too: to decide your fate!
When rolled in conjunction, two D10's act as percentile dice. Generally in this scenario, you'll find two 10-sided dice in your arsenal that match in colour. One will run the numbers in 10's, the other will be the expected 1-10. Say the one lands on 30, and the other lands on 7, you rolled a 37. Now this may be good or it may be very, very bad. It all depends on the percentage chance your DM has decided on for this particular roll.

The D12, this rather lovely piece of equipment is mainly used for two handed weapons. Say a rather aggressive Gnoll runs up to you, you swing your Great Axe and providing you hit you roll that D12. Some Great Weapons use 2D6 instead, but all this changes is your probability of rolling a certain number.

The D20, this is the main dice you will be rolling when playing Dungeons & Dragons. It allows you to know if you've successfully persuaded someone to your way of thinking, how well you've hidden from those big scary Town Guard when you've been stealing. Whether or not you've successfully picked a lock, remembered something from your past, the list is near endless. Most importantly though, it allows you to determine when you get to attack and whether or not you've struck true.

The D100, sometimes you'll come across these behemoth dice, they simply act as your percentile die. Typically when your DM asks you to roll a percentage chance, they'll say "Roll a D100". All they mean is roll your two D10s, but you can roll a D100 if you've got one.
